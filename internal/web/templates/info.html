Understanding the Statistics

This page explains the metrics used throughout the EPUB Reader dashboard.

Word Metrics

Total Words

The total count of all words in the book, including repeated words. For example, "The cat sat on the mat" contains 6 words.

Unique Words

The count of distinct word forms. Each different spelling counts as a separate word, so "try" and "trying" are counted as two unique words. This is also called the "vocabulary size."

Vocabulary Richness

The ratio of unique words to total words (Unique / Total). A higher value indicates more varied vocabulary. Also known as the type-token ratio (TTR).

  • 0.10 - 0.20: Typical for longer texts (vocabulary naturally repeats)
  • 0.20 - 0.40: Moderately varied vocabulary
  • 0.40+: Very rich vocabulary (often in shorter texts or poetry)

Hapax Legomena

Words that appear exactly once in the text. The term comes from Greek meaning "said only once." A high count of hapax legomena indicates the author uses many rare or unique words. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Average Word Length

The mean number of characters per word. Longer average word length often correlates with more complex or technical writing.

Readability Metrics

Readability Score (Flesch Reading Ease)

A score from 0-100 indicating how easy the text is to read. Higher scores mean easier reading. Based on sentence length and syllables per word. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Average Sentence Length

The mean number of words per sentence. Longer sentences are generally harder to parse and remember.

  • <15 words: Short, punchy sentences (thriller/action style)
  • 15-25 words: Standard prose
  • >25 words: Complex, literary style

Total Syllables / Average Syllables per Word

Syllable count is used in readability calculations. More syllables per word typically means more complex vocabulary.

Structure Metrics

Total Sentences

Count of sentences in the book, detected by punctuation (., !, ?).

Total Paragraphs

Count of text blocks separated by blank lines or paragraph breaks.

Average Paragraph Length

Mean number of sentences per paragraph. Shorter paragraphs create faster pacing; longer paragraphs suit detailed exposition.

Style Metrics

Dialogue Ratio

The percentage of text that appears within quotation marks. Higher dialogue ratios indicate more character interaction and conversation.

  • <10%: Narrative-heavy, descriptive prose
  • 10-30%: Balanced mix of narrative and dialogue
  • >30%: Dialogue-driven (common in mysteries, thrillers)

Corpus Analysis

When viewing an author's page, you see aggregated statistics across all their books in your library. These "corpus" metrics help identify an author's overall style:

  • Average Vocabulary Richness: Mean TTR across books
  • Average Readability: Mean Flesch score
  • Average Sentence Length: Typical sentence complexity
  • Average Dialogue Ratio: How much dialogue they typically use
